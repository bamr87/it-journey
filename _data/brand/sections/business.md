# Business — Section Guide

> Voice/values live in `_data/brand/`. State only business-specific deltas here.

## Audience

Practitioners and educators thinking about the business side of tech — ERP, the
economics of tooling, finance/accounting workflows — who want the "so what for the
work" connection.

## Voice profile

`concept-essay` (see `../voice.md`). **Section delta:** make a clear argument and
support it with evidence or first-hand experience; land on a practical implication.

## Tone

Analytical and grounded, not consultant-speak. Bridge abstract concepts to concrete
outcomes (budgets, workflows, decisions).

## Structure

Inherits the `posts.instructions.md` skeleton, but argument-led rather than
step-by-step: claim → evidence → implication. Tables for comparisons.

## Do / Don't

- **Do:** cite sources for market/industry claims; tie the idea back to a decision a
  reader could make; keep it open-source-minded (OSAP).
- **Don't:** hype ("game-changing", "revolutionary"); buzzword strings; claims with
  no evidence behind them.

## Example openings

- "ERP isn't software — it's the operating model of a company expressed in code.
  That reframing changes who should own the rollout."
- "Spreadsheets run more of the economy than any database. Here's what that means for
  how we teach 'programming'."
